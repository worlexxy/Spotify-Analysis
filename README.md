# Spotify-Analysis

**INTRODUCTION**

This analysis explores music performance on Spotify in 2024, focusing on how tracks, albums, and artists perform based on listener engagement metrics such as streams, popularity, playlist inclusion, and track scores.sotify

**DATA VISUALIZATION**

The Spotify Streaming Dashboard was created using Microsoft Excel. The dataset was first cleaned by removing duplicates, correcting errors, and standardizing formats. Pivot Tables were then used to summarize streaming metrics such as top artists, albums, tracks, playlist counts, and score distributions.

Different chart types were used to visualize the data effectively, including Doughnut Charts for Top Albums, Top Tracks, and Explicit Content Distribution, Bar Charts for Top Artists and Playlist Counts, Column Charts for Track Distribution by Score, and Horizontal Bar Charts to compare streaming performance across artists and tracks. Slicers, formatting, and Spotify-themed colors were also applied to create an interactive and visually engaging dashboard.

**DATA SPLITTLING AND PRE-PROCESSING**

**Data Cleaning:** Duplicates were removed, errors were corrected, and formats (names, dates, numbers) were standardized. 

**Data Transformations:** New metrics (e.g., total streams per artist/album) were created, values were normalized, and tracks were grouped into score ranges.

**Data Splitting:**

**Independent variables:** Track Name, Album Name, Artist

**Dependent variables:** Track Score, Spotify Popularity, Streams, Playlist Count, All-Time Rank, Explicit, Release Date

**GENERAL OBSERVATION**

The Eminem Show ranks among the Top 3 albums by streams, while Without Me and Lose Yourself remain some of the most streamed tracks, reflecting both current engagement and lasting popularity. Without Me also stands out in the Top 5 by playlist count (397,228 adds), showing strong user-driven inclusion in curated lists. All analyzed tracks are explicit, reinforcing Eminem’s identity as an artist with mature-themed content. Several tracks fall into higher listener score ranges (24.4–29.4), suggesting consistent favorability among listeners.

Taylor Swift continues her dominance on Spotify in 2024 with 7.6B+ total streams, ranking as one of the top global artists. Her albums Midnights (1.8B+), 1989 (Deluxe) (1.3B+), and You Need To Calm Down (935M+) highlight her strong catalog performance. Tracks like Style (1.2B+), You Belong With Me (935M+), and I Knew You Were Trouble (842M+) remain highly popular, combining both longevity and fan engagement. Playlist presence is strong, with I Knew You Were Trouble leading (110K+ adds), supported by Style and You Belong With Me. With a mix of clean (9) and explicit (2) tracks, she appeals to diverse audiences, while listener scores consistently fall in the favourable 24.4–29.4 range. Overall, Swift’s streaming success reflects both the enduring strength of her classics and the wide appeal of her newer releases.

⁠In 2024, The Weeknd was Spotify's top streamed artist with 6.17 billion streams, led by hit albums like Starboy and The Highlights (Deluxe), top tracks including Reminder and Heartless, strong playlist presence with songs like Pray For Me, and a majority of his popular tracks marked as explicit, with most track ratings falling between 24.4 and 29.4.

Kanye West shows strong and consistent performance, with his top three albums (808s & Heartbreak, The Life of Pablo, and Yeezus) all surpassing 900M streams. His leading tracks — Heartless (1.3B+), Father Stretch My Hands Pt. 1 (1.1B+), and Bound 2 (948M+) mirror the album success, confirming their role as key streaming drivers. Listener scores lean positive, with most tracks in the 24.4–29.4 range, reflecting strong reception. Heartless stands out across metrics, with 261K+ playlist adds, making it his most influential track. Most of his top tracks are explicit, consistent with his style, yet still achieve high rankings. With 4B+ total streams, Kanye West ranks among the top artists of 2024, powered by both catalog longevity and playlist driven visibility.

Asake stands out as a top performer with 153M+ total streams. His albums Work of Art (84.6M) and Amapiano (69M) dominate his catalog, driven by the success of their title tracks Lonely At The Top (84.6M) and Amapiano (69M). Playlist presence is a key factor, with Amapiano featured in 21K+ playlists and Lonely At The Top in 13K+, fueling visibility and engagement. His track distribution shows wide variance, with one song in the lower score range (19.4–24.4) and another in the higher range (49.4–54.4), reflecting both underperformance and standout success. Content balance is evident, as his two tracks split evenly between explicit and non explicit, broadening audience reach. Overall, Asake’s streaming power is anchored by a few high performing hits that drive both his popularity and playlist traction.

**CONCLUSION**

This analysis shows that Spotify performance in 2024 was strongly influenced by streams, playlist inclusion, popularity, and listener engagement. Artists such as Taylor Swift, The Weeknd, Eminem, Kanye West, and Asake maintained strong streaming success through high playlist visibility and consistent listener engagement. The findings highlight the importance of playlist placement, catalog longevity, and audience reach in driving music performance, while also showing how data analytics can support personalization, artist growth, and better streaming strategies.
